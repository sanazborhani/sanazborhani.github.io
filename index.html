About

I am a licensed Professional Engineer with a PhD in Water Resources Engineering and more than a decade of experience delivering hydrologic and hydraulic analyses for bridges, culverts, floodplains, and climate resilience projects. I work with HEC‑RAS, SRH‑2D, HEC‑HMS, SWMM, and ArcGIS, and I build Python tools to automate watershed and modeling workflows.

Projects

AECOM (2025–Present) — Bridge & Climate Resilience
  • H&H Report Reviews — AMTRAK Culverts (CT): Reviewed H&H reports for multiple culverts (Enfield, Hartford, Windsor Locks).
  • Onondaga Creek (Syracuse, NY): Preliminary hydraulics + scour analysis to support design-build proposal for replacing five structures.
  • Mystic River Pedestrian Bridge (Everett, MA): Peak flow estimation using Massachusetts streamflow regression equations; climate change analysis for future sea level rise.
  • Route 20 over Connecticut River (Springfield, MA): 2D steady-state SRH‑2D hydrodynamic modeling to produce velocities and water levels for scour calculations.
  • Cranes Hollow Rd Bridge over Evans Kill (Amsterdam, NY): Hydrology via USGS StreamStats + 1D HEC‑RAS hydraulic modeling.
  • Wagners Hollow Rd Bridge over Caroga Creek (Palatine, NY): Hydrology via USGS StreamStats + 1D HEC‑RAS hydraulic modeling.
  • Kensico Reservoir Drainage Basin (Westchester, NY): Analyzed observed concentration data and developed methodology for PFAS loading.
DOT / Rail
Jacobs (2022–2025) — Flood Risk, FEMA, Bridge Scour
  • Green Brook Flood Risk Management (NJ): Revised permitting HEC‑RAS model; created floodplain maps in ArcGIS; scour analysis + ACB countermeasures for Route 28 bridge.
  • Hamilton Rd Bridge over Conrail/CSX (NJ): Hydrology using NRCS TR‑55; HEC‑RAS 6.3 steady-state model for flood elevation impacts.
  • HEC‑RAS 2D Rain‑on‑Grid (Climate Change): Evaluated historical (1999, 2020) and projected (2100) rainfall events for floodplain assessment.
  • Route 23 Bridge over Mill Brook (NJ): PondPack NRCS methodology for peak flows (current + 2100); 1D HEC‑RAS + SRH‑2D for split flow conditions.
  • Newton–Weston Bridge Bundle (Boston, MA): 1D steady HEC‑RAS + unsteady SRH‑2D; scour analysis per MassDOT LRFD Bridge Manual; FEMA no‑rise analysis.
  • Route 27 Culvert over Northern Tributary to Ten Mile Run (NJ): 1D HEC‑RAS split-flow modeling; PondPack NRCS peak flow (current + 2100).
  • Route 30 Bridge over Duck Thorofare (Atlantic City, NJ): Unsteady 2D modeling in SMS/SRH‑2D; tidal hydrology/scour (HEC‑25); scour per HEC‑18/25.
  • Route 88 Bridge over Beaver Dam Creek (NJ): Hydrology (NRCS, ArcGIS, PondPack) + HEC‑RAS Mapper; stage-storage routing; detailed fluvial + tidal scour (HEC‑18/20/23/25).
  • Route 94 Bridge over Jacksonburg Creek (NJ): HEC‑RAS models for existing/temporary/proposed; scour per HEC‑18/20/23; NJDEP FHACAR/SWM regs; alternatives & downstream impact checks.
  • SR 0012 Crossing over Bernhart Creek (PA): Updated hydrology and selected TR‑55 method based on watershed characteristics.
  • Tremley Point Connector Rd over Rahway River (NJ): SRH‑2D floodplain model using fluvial peak flows from unsteady HEC‑RAS; revised 1D steady HEC‑RAS for impacts.
Flood Risk
Research & Modeling (2013–2022) — Urban Flooding, Morphodynamics
  • Drexel (2021–2022): 2D flood modeling using PCSWMM for Camden, NJ; downscaled extreme precipitation in ultra-urban H&H models (Cross‑RISA partnership).
  • Montalto & Rothstein / eDesign Dynamics (2020–2022): 1D–2D PCSWMM + ArcGIS model for dredging impacts on Puerto Rico flood inundation maps; HEC‑RAS/HEC‑HMS/GeoHMS for San Juan Estuary building-site modeling.
  • Virginia Tech (2019–2020): Watershed delineation and stream stability/stormwater practice research; Python frequency analysis for effective discharge (Po River, Italy).
  • University of South Carolina (2013–2019): Flood hazards, levee breach and dam failure modeling; river morphology and sediment/scour modeling; hydrologic modeling and lab facility improvements.
